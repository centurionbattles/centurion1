Centurion — How to Play

Quick Start
  1. White moves first in odd rounds, Black in even rounds (auto-alternates).
  2. You have 30s per move. If time runs out, you lose your turn.
  3. Capture pieces to earn points; capturing the King ends the round immediately.
  4. After 4 rounds, the higher Total Score wins. Ties trigger a 5th tie-breaker round.
Board & Setup

Board is 10×10. Each side has: R, N, B, W (Diplomat), Q, K, W, B, N, R and a second rank of pawns with two Archers (A).

Turn & Timer
  • 30s per move; timer turns red and flashes under 10s.
  • Timer theme: Black (black + cream), White (white + grey).
Piece Movement
  • P Pawn: 1 forward; 2 from start; diagonal capture. Promotes on last rank (Q/R/B/A/N).
  • R Rook: orthogonal, any squares (no jump).
  • N Knight: L-shape (jumps).
  • B Bishop: diagonal, any squares (no jump).
  • Q Queen: rook + bishop.
  • K King: 1 square any direction.
  • A Archer: exactly 2 squares in any direction (jumps).
  • W Diplomat: moves like a King; can convert (see below).

Diplomat Conversion
  • Click your Diplomat (W), then an adjacent enemy (non-King).
  • You gain its points immediately, then the piece switches sides.
Promotion

Pawns promote on the last rank to Q, R, B, A, N.

Rounds & Winning
  • Round ends on King capture.
  • Round points are added to Total Score, then round points reset.
  • Most points after 4 rounds wins (tie → 5th round).
UI Tips
  • Valid moves show as green dots.
  • Click a different own piece to switch selection; click empty to deselect.
FAQ

Do Archers jump? Yes, exactly two squares in any direction.

Can the Diplomat convert a King? No.

Who starts first? White in odd rounds, Black in even rounds.
